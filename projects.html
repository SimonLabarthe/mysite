Predicting FIFA26 results

FORTHECOMING ABSTRACT

This study develops and evaluates statistical learning models to predict home team goal counts in international football matches, with specific application to the 2026 FIFA World Cup. Using a comprehensive dataset of over 50,000 international matches from 1990 to 2026, I integrate historical performance metrics, Elo ratings, geographical features, and country-level socioeconomic indicators.

Do left-wing or female representatives reduce violence against women? RDD evidence from Brazil

2026.06.15 ABSTRACT

A growing set of literature explores the effect of the election of female can didates on social outcomes without taking ideology into account. This essay aims to fulfill this gap by examining whether political affiliation contribute—alongside, and cumulatively—to the reduction of Interpersonal Violence against Women(IPVAW). Combining data from the electionsBR package and DATA SUS/SINAN on brazilian municipalities between 2016 and 2025, the article follow a sharp Regression Discontinuity Design (RDD) to explore whether barely winning elections have an impact on the IPVAW rate. The results reinforce Bochenkova et al. (2023) previous findings : gender matters more, while political orientation has limited effects.

Militarised Interstate Dispute Predicted Probabilities Dataset

2026.05.15 ABSTRACT

This paper introduces a novel public dataset providing ex ante predicted probabilities of Militarized Interstate Dispute (MID) onset at the dyadic and monadic levels for the period 1992–2012. While existing quantitative re- search on political violence typically relies on realized conflict outcomes as explanatory variables, theoretical frameworks emphasize the importance of anticipated conflict risk in shaping state behavior regarding arming, alliance formation, and concessions. To address this gap, I estimate four statistical learning classifiers (logistic regression, elastic net, random forest, and gra- dient boosting) using 91 predictors. The random forest model achieves the strongest discriminative performance on a held-out test set (AUC = 0.994, precision = 0.889), substantially outperforming parametric alternatives. The resulting dataset offers researchers a theoretically grounded measure of latent conflict propensity that can be incorporated into models of militarization, preventive war, nationalist sentiment, and related phenomena.
